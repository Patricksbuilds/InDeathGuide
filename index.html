What is this game

The Afterlife Has Fallen

In Death: Unchained is a roguelite archery game built exclusively for the Meta Quest platform — Quest 2, Quest 3, and Quest 3S. Developed by Superbright, a small studio based in Wrocław, Poland, it is the definitive evolution of the original In Death (2018, PC VR), rebuilt from the ground up for standalone VR with years of free updates, new worlds, new enemies, and a thriving competitive leaderboard community.

You play as a lone archer cast into a godless afterlife — a crumbling medieval heaven where fallen angels, monks, knights, demons, and creatures of the Abyss have taken dominion. Armed with a bow (and eventually a crossbow), you fight your way through procedurally generated cathedrals, floating platforms, catacombs, and hellscapes. Every run is different. Every death matters. And every death makes you stronger.

The game draws its aesthetic and lore from Dante's Divine Comedy and Milton's Paradise Lost — the world names are not accidental. This is not a heavy metal hell. It is something stranger, grander, and more melancholy: a paradise gone wrong, populated by beings that were once holy.

The 2024 Quest 3 update brought a substantial visual overhaul — approximately 30% increased display resolution, 90Hz support, enhanced stained glass lighting, enemy shadows, and more complex reflective surfaces — making this the most visually accomplished version of the game to date.

Progression

Four Worlds. One Brutal Loop.

You begin every run in the Sanctuary — a safe starting hub where you choose your weapon and arrows. From there, the afterlife opens before you. Complete one full loop and Purgatory II awaits: the same worlds, harder enemies, no mercy.

World I

Purgatory

Boss — Anakim the Abominable

Gothic cathedral spires jutting from floating stone platforms. The entry point for every archer. Knights, monks, archers, and abominations patrol the cloisters. Learn the bow here. Master the teleport. Everything that follows is harder.

World II

Desolation

Boss — Anakim the Abominable

An alternate route added in free DLC — mysterious outdoor catacombs and confined underground passages. Four enemy types found nowhere else, including fast-moving aerial threats that punish hesitation. An alternate path to the first boss.

World III

Paradise Lost

Boss — Gabriel the Fallen

Marble-sheened corridors and open heavenly architecture where the beauty of creation has curdled into something dangerous. Banshees appear here. The Azure Fury arrow is unlocked by surviving long enough to kill nine of them.

World IV

The Abyss

Boss — Asmodeus the Destroyer

The deep end. Hell made architecture. The game's ultimate challenge and the gate to Purgatory II. Defeating Asmodeus unlocks the Wrath of God arrow — the most powerful in the game, and your reward for surviving the worst the afterlife can offer.

Core mechanic

Death Is the Beginning

In Death: Unchained is a roguelite — a game where permanent death is a feature, not a punishment. Each run you push further, unlock achievements, and accumulate progress that carries forward even when you fall. The further you get, the harder the enemies become. The more you play, the more arrows, enemy types, and challenges unlock.

⚔️

Enter the Run

Each run begins in the Sanctuary. Choose your weapon and starting arrows. The world ahead is procedurally generated — never the same twice.

🏹

Fight & Explore

Clear enemies, find arrow pickups, visit the Reliquary to spend gold, and push through each zone. Leave no enemy behind — kills build your score.

💀

Die

You will die. Often. But every kill, every achievement milestone, and every gold piece looted in that run contributes permanently to your progress.

✦

Rise Stronger

Achievements unlock new arrows, new enemy types, new challenges. Your overall progress percentage climbs. The game genuinely grows as you do.

True Mastery

Arsenal

Choose Your Weapon

All trick arrows work identically across both weapons. Your choice is about playstyle — precision and power versus speed and aggression. Switch between them at weapon shrines in the Sanctuary and at the start of each level.

The Longbow

Available from the start

The purist's weapon. Draw strength determines damage — a full draw headshot at range hits significantly harder than a rushed close shot. Rewards patience, accuracy, and a consistent anchor point. At range, no weapon in the game is more satisfying. The natural choice for those who want precision to matter.

The Crossbow

Unlocked after 101 kills

Rapid fire, fixed maximum damage per bolt, loaded with a single hand motion then aimed like a pistol. Sidesteps the Quest's inside-out tracking limitations on wide bow draws. Favoured by high-score leaderboard players and experienced archers who want throughput over precision. The shield and teleport shard controls swap hands when using the crossbow.

Alternative Mode

Game Mode

Siege of Heaven

Not every session needs to be a full roguelite run. Siege of Heaven is a wave-defence mode — you hold a fixed position and fight off escalating waves of enemies rather than navigating procedurally generated worlds. It has its own separate achievement track (72 achievements), its own leaderboard, and was designed as a more accessible entry point. Chapter II maps added new unique mechanics to each Siege map.

  • Stand your ground — no teleport navigation required
  • Escalating enemy waves with elite variants
  • 72 separate achievements from the main game
  • Separate seasonal leaderboard
  • Crossbow not available in this mode
  • Good for shorter sessions or practising arrow types
